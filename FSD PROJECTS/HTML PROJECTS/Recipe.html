Black Coffee Recipe

Ingredients

  • Coffee Beans
  • Water
  • Sugar

Instructions

  1. Grind your beans: Use a medium-coarse grind for a balanced flavor.
  2. Measure your grounds: The general rule is 1 tablespoon of grounds for every 6 ounces of water. Adjust to your preference.
  3. Heat your water: Bring water to a boil, then let it cool for about 30 seconds to 1 minute. The ideal temperature is around 200 degrees Fahrenheit (93 degrees Celsius).
  4. Steep: Allow the coffee to steep for 3-4 minutes. The longer you steep, the stronger the coffee will be.
  5. Serve: Once steeped, remove the grounds from the coffee. Pour the brewed coffee into a mug and enjoy!

Optional

For a stronger cup, use a finer grind and increase the steep time. To adjust the acidity, experiment with different water temperatures.
